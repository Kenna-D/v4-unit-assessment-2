Skills Check 2

Tolkien created his stories to fit the world he created, and not the other way around. Okay, he started off with the Fall of Gondolin, but aside from that, he was mainly focused on how the stories tie into the world, not the other way around.

That's how the Lord of the Rings came about. Tolkien wanted to release the Silmarillion, but the publishers were skeptical about it, especially with post-war paper shortages. So, he wrote the Lord of the Rings as a new "story about Hobbits". But the world of Middle Earth had been created long before LotR was conceived.

Compare that to other fantasy authors. Say, I am re-reading "the Witcher" right now. While it's a great anchise, it's clear that the world is only as deep as it's necessary for the story to be played out. We know almost nothing about the history of particular kingdoms. Another example would be G.R.R. Martin. He once said that in an email, a fan asked him for more details about the Valyrian language. He responded that he doesn't know anything more about it, because he created it only to the extent to which it was necessary for the story.

Conclusion, Tolkien's world seems more genuine because it takes precedebce over the stories that take place inside of it. Most other authors don't do it.
